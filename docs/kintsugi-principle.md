# The Kintsugi Principle

The governing philosophy of this project, carried over from SoiKio and expressed here in fresh terms.

## The idea

*Kintsugi* is the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery with gold — the break is highlighted, not hidden, and the mended piece is valued more for it. Applied to analysis:

> **Disagreement is signal, not error. The cracks in a thesis are not flaws to be smoothed away — they are the most valuable information you have. Gild them.**

Most research collapses competing perspectives into one confident answer, hiding the seams. This project does the opposite: it **surfaces every crack, ranks it, and makes the map of disagreement the headline output** — not a footnote beneath a tidy verdict.

## Why it matters for red-teaming a thesis

When you bring your own investment thesis to be stress-tested, a smooth "looks fine" is the least useful possible answer. What you need is the **gold in the cracks**: precisely where the public evidence contradicts you, where a claim is unsupported, and where the thesis is most fragile. Those cracks are the product. The Bear and Caution agents exist to find them; the CIO exists to gild and rank them.

## How it shows up in the system

- **The conflict map is the primary output**, not the verdict. Every point where the evidence (or a stance) disagrees with the thesis is a crack on the map.
- **Cracks are ranked by severity.** High-severity cracks — ones that could break the thesis — are marked ⚡ and demand attention.
- **Each crack is traceable and resolvable.** A crack names the claim under test, the type (contradicted / unsupported / vulnerable), its evidence (cited), and *what would resolve it* — so disagreement points to action, not just doubt.
- **Consensus is treated with suspicion.** A thesis that survives with no cracks may simply not have been tested from enough angles; the system says so rather than rewarding it with false confidence.

## Relationship to the other principles

Kintsugi is the "why" behind **conflict-as-signal**. It sits alongside cite-or-silence (no claim without a source) and analysis-not-advice (no BUY/SELL/HOLD): together they describe a tool that won't fabricate, won't decide for you, and won't hide the disagreement that matters most.
