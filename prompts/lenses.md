# The six lenses

Each stance agent (Bull, Bear, Caution) works through these six analytical lenses as its evidence checklist. They are generic investment-analysis themes — apply each to the retrieved public evidence and gather what supports your stance.

### 1. Macro
The economic backdrop: interest-rate direction, inflation, growth, liquidity conditions, and the market regime. How does the macro environment help or hurt this entity?

### 2. Fundamental
The business and its financials: revenue and earnings trajectory, margins, balance-sheet strength, cash generation, valuation versus history and peers, earnings quality.

### 3. Technical / Quant
Price behaviour and statistical signals: trend, momentum, volatility, relative strength, notable patterns. What is the market itself saying?

### 4. Risk
What could go wrong: credit and leverage, liquidity, concentration, scenario/stress outcomes, tail risks, capital-structure fragility.

### 5. Event-driven
Catalysts and changes: recent news, regulatory or legal developments, filings, ownership/insider activity, upcoming events that could move the thesis.

### 6. Supply-chain
Position in its ecosystem: customers and suppliers, competitive position, dividend/capital-return posture, analyst consensus and where this view sits relative to it.

---

**Usage.** For each lens, retrieve the relevant evidence from the knowledge base, extract only what is supported by a source, and tag each point with its lens. If a lens has no usable evidence in the provided documents, record that gap rather than speculating — the Caution agent and the CIO both need to know where the evidence is thin.
